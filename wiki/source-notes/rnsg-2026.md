# RNSG: A Range-Aware Graph Index for Efficient Range-Filtered Approximate Nearest Neighbor Search

## Summary

Zou et al. address range-filtered ANN (RFANN), where each object has both a vector and a numeric attribute and queries restrict results to an attribute interval. The paper proposes Range-aware Relative Neighborhood Graph (RRNG) as an ideal graph model and RNSG as a practical approximation.

## Key Contributions

- Defines RRNG, extending MRNG-style proximity reasoning with attribute proximity.
- Proves monotonic searchability for RRNG and a structural heredity property: range-induced subgraphs remain valid RRNGs.
- Argues that this heredity property lets one graph support many range filters instead of building multiple range-specific indexes.
- Proposes RNSG as an efficient approximation using spatial and attribute candidate neighborhoods plus range-aware pruning.
- Adds an RNSG query procedure with entry-node generation for RFANN.

## Reported Results

- Reports higher query performance than strong RFANN baselines such as iRangeGraph and UNIFY at matched recall in the paper's benchmark suite.
- Reports more compact deployed indexes than multi-index range-filtering baselines.
- Reports lower construction cost than compared range-index baselines, with RNSG build time split into reusable KNNG construction and RNSG-specific pruning.

## Importance

RNSG extends the proximity-graph thread into vector-database semantics. It directly addresses a weakness of pure spatial graph indexes: after filtering removes nodes, the remaining graph may lose the connectivity and monotonic paths that graph search needs.

## Limits And Open Questions

- The PDF is an arXiv 2026 version with placeholder PVLDB reference/DOI fields, so venue metadata should be treated as unstable until checked against the final proceedings.
- Exact RRNG construction is expensive; practical RNSG is an approximation and can differ from rebuilt interval-specific graphs.
- The work focuses on numeric range filters; other predicate types may need different heredity conditions.
