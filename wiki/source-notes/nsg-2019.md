# Fast Approximate Nearest Neighbor Search With the Navigating Spreading-out Graph

## Summary

This paper proposes NSG as a practical graph index for large-scale ANN search. It starts from a theoretical target graph (MRNG) and builds a practical approximation that emphasizes connectivity, short paths, low out-degree, and reduced index size.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces MRNG as a graph with low expected search complexity (close to logarithmic, by the paper's analysis).
- Proposes NSG as a practical approximation of MRNG with lower indexing complexity.
- Uses the broader approximate KNN graph and candidate-pool lineage that includes [NN-Descent](../entities/nn-descent.md) and [DPG](../entities/diversified-proximity-graph.md).
- Targets billion-node scale and reports production adoption in Taobao's search engine.

## Reported Results

- Claims NSG significantly outperforms tested baselines in search performance and memory efficiency in the paper's benchmarks.
- Positions NSG as a scalable successor to earlier graph approaches that were strong at million scale but weak at billion scale.

## Limits And Open Questions

- NSG remains an in-memory-leaning graph approach in most common deployments; storage-tier and disaggregated-memory behavior are addressed more directly in later system papers.
- The paper emphasizes graph design; practical gains can still depend heavily on implementation and hardware path.
